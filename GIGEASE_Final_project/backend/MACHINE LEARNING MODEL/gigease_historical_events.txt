Chennai Flood Events - Historical Record
2015 December: Catastrophic flooding across Chennai. 490mm rain in 24 hours. Velachery, Adyar, Sholinganallur submerged for 5-7 days. Chembarambakkam dam released 29,000 cusecs. Estimated 300+ fatalities. All delivery operations ceased for 12 days.
2017 November: Cyclone Ockhi brought 180mm rainfall. Coastal zones affected. Adyar river overflowed. 3-day disruption.
2019 October: Northeast monsoon early onset. Velachery waterlogged for 48 hours. T Nagar commercial areas flooded. IMD Red Alert issued.
2021 November: 200mm+ rainfall over 3 days. Velachery and Tambaram severely affected. NDMA Level 2 alert. Delivery platforms suspended operations in 4 zones for 5 days.
2023 December: Cyclone Michaung made landfall near Chennai. 250mm in 36 hours. All 8 zones disrupted. NDMA Level 3. Complete delivery shutdown for 4 days. Recovery took 8 days in low-lying zones.
RSMD Events:
2020 February: Anti-CAA protests in T Nagar and Anna Nagar. Commercial shutdowns for 3 days. Delivery workers unable to operate due to road blockades.
2022 September: Tamil Nadu bandh called by opposition parties. Guindy and T Nagar fully shut. Sporadic violence in some areas. 2-day disruption.
2024 March: Water crisis protests in Tambaram and Porur. Partial shutdowns. 1-day disruption with reduced order volumes.
