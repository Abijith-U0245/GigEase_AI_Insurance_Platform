GigEase Case Studies and Expected Behaviors

Example 1: Genuine STFI Flood Claim (The Velachery Submersion)
Arun S runs deliveries in Velachery. During the first week of November, extremely heavy monsoonal rainfall dumped 187mm of rain in a single day, causing the NDMA to issue a Level 4 flood alert. Because Velachery sits in low elevation with poor drainage, Arun's typical deliveries ground to an absolute halt. He logged 4 days of zero income. His expected income (w_expected) stood at a stable 4800 INR, but his actual weekly earnings completely plummeted to just 1536 INR. This huge drop safely surpassed the 60% parametric loss threshold. The GigEase system automatically confirmed the STFI Level 4 event and applied a high 0.80 payout beta. Without a dispute or claims adjuster, GigEase automatically dispersed 1243.50 INR to his account, recognizing a true verified disaster.

Example 2: Genuine RSMD Claim (The T Nagar Curfew)
Deepa M routinely delivers goods in the commercial hub of T Nagar. In July, widespread protests rapidly erupted surrounding a local administrative dispute, resulting in an immediate 48-hour police-enforced curfew. Multiple news sources reported the sudden bandh, triggering an rsmd_news_score of 0.92, successfully crossing the two-source corroboration rule alongside maximum google maps congestion metrics. Her income, normally 3200 INR per week, crashed down to 1800 INR because the surrounding restaurants were forbidden from opening. Since this represented a steep drop below the 60% expected boundary and the systemic severity scored a 3, a 0.65 beta was applied. Deepa was credited with an automatic payout of 839.28 INR. 

Example 3: GPS Fraud Override (The Location Spoofing Rejection)
Ganesh R primarily operates in T Nagar. In early December, a city-wide STFI flood warning was correctly triggered. Anticipating effortless parametric payouts, Ganesh purposely engaged a GPS spoofing app directly on his mobile device to artificially inflate his reported distance logs by 4.5 kilometers per trip, generating fake “waterlogged detour” trips while physically remaining at his apartment. However, the system's Layer 1 backend cross-referenced the spoofed GPS logs strictly against his cellular tower pings, identifying the blatant 4.5km mismatch. Despite the active STFI flood alert legally providing the grounds for a claim, the system enforced an AUTO_REJECT fraud_action. claim_triggered was aggressively overwritten to 0, completely voiding his potential claim irrespective of the authentic severe weather. No money was dispersed.
